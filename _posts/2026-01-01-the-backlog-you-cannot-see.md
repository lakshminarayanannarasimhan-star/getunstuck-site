## The Problem

Every leader I have worked with carries more than their calendar shows.

There are the meetings, the decisions, the deliverables — all visible, all tracked. And then there is the other thing: the conversations not yet had, the commitments quietly accumulating weight, the choices that keep getting deferred without a decision being made.

This is the invisible backlog.

## Why It Matters

The invisible backlog is not a productivity problem. It is a clarity problem. You cannot close what you cannot see. You cannot decide on what has no name.

The cost is not time — it is cognitive load. Every unnamed item is a background process running, consuming the mental resource you need for the work that actually requires your best thinking.

## The Practice

The solution is not a better task manager. It is the discipline of naming — regularly, deliberately, without judgment.

Name what you are carrying. Look at it clearly. Then decide: close it, plan it, delegate it, or consciously defer it.

That is the entire system. The practice is doing it consistently enough that nothing accumulates long enough to become weight.
