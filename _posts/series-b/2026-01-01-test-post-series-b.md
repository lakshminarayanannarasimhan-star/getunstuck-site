There is a particular kind of stuck that looks like success from the outside.

The leader is delivering. Results are good. Performance reviews are positive. Compensation reflects the value they are adding. And they are going nowhere. Not stalling out — they are still moving — but moving on a flat line rather than a rising one. Getting better at a job that is not getting bigger.

This is the Compression Zone. And it is where most capable people in technology-services organisations spend the majority of their careers.

## What execution actually earns

Execution earns compensation. This is not a criticism — it is a description of the exchange. You deliver reliably, you get paid fairly. The organisation does not owe you more than that for delivery alone. Delivery is the floor of the agreement, not the ceiling.

The problem is that most leadership development thinking — and most of the advice given to ambitious people inside organisations — is focused on getting better at delivery. Do more. Do it faster. Do it more visibly. The implicit theory is that enough execution compounds into something else.

It does not. Execution compounds into more execution. Better performance at a job is not the same as a larger job. And the leaders who cross from delivery into indispensability are not the ones who out-executed everyone else. They did something structurally different.

## The six-level arc

The arc runs like this:

**Execution earns compensation.** The fair exchange for reliable delivery. Most people live here, permanently.

**Trust earns access.** When people trust you with consequential things, they let you into the rooms where consequential decisions are made. Access is not given — it is earned by making people confident that bringing you inside is safe.

**Influence earns a seat.** A seat is not presence in the room. A seat is a voice that changes what the room decides. Influence is the difference between attending a meeting and shaping its outcome.

**Indispensability earns participation.** Participation means being brought into decisions before they are made, not after. It means your absence creates a visible gap. This is the level where departure costs the organisation something it cannot immediately replace.

**Ownership earns control.** Control over direction, over resources, over the shape of the work itself. This is not granted — it is taken by demonstrating that you can hold the system, not just operate within it.

**Growth earns a premium.** Above-market compensation, expanded scope, access to the next platform. Growth is the consequence of the levels above it being genuinely installed — not asserted.

Most people in technology-services organisations are stuck between Execution and Trust. A smaller number make it to Influence. Fewer still reach Indispensability in any genuine sense. And the reason is not talent. The reason is that each crossing requires a different move — and most people respond to being stuck by doing more of the move that got them to the current level.

## The Compression Zone

Between Execution and Trust there is a zone where many careers stall permanently. Call it the Compression Zone: you are delivering at high levels, the work is visible, and nothing is opening. No new rooms. No larger scope. The same responsibilities, better executed.

The Compression Zone is not a talent problem. The people in it are often the most technically capable individuals in the organisation. The problem is structural: execution, at scale, becomes invisible. The organisation expects it. It is priced in. Adding more of it does not change the price.

> The move out of the Compression Zone is not to deliver more. It is to become the person whose *judgment* the organisation trusts — not just whose delivery it relies on.

That is the Trust Crossing. It is not a performance threshold. It is a relationship threshold. And most people try to cross it by performing harder, which is exactly the wrong move.

## Why the crossing matters now

In the technology-services and GenAI context, the Compression Zone is widening. Execution is being automated, augmented, and commoditised faster than at any previous point. The work that earned a career ten years ago is increasingly handleable by tools that do not need sleep.

This is not a threat to address with better execution. It is a structural change that makes the crossings above execution — to trust, influence, indispensability, ownership — not optional. They are now the floor of what a sustainable career requires.

The leaders who understand this early enough will not be surprised when the organisation revalues the currency they have been spending. They will already be spending a different one.

## The Drill — Three Questions

These are not performance questions. Answer them about your actual situation, not your aspirational one.

**1. Identify your current level.**
Place yourself on the arc: Execution, Trust, Influence, Indispensability, Ownership, or Growth. Write one sentence of evidence — not aspiration. What specifically happened in the last 90 days that places you there?

**2. Name the crossing.**
What is the next level above where you are? What is the specific, single move required to make that crossing — not "work harder" or "be more visible", but the actual different thing?
*Write the move. Not the intention. The move.*

**3. Find the gap.**
If you are in the Compression Zone: what is the one relationship in your organisation where trust has not yet been established — and where establishing it would open the next room?
*Write the name, the gap, and the first action. Owner: you. Date: this week.*
