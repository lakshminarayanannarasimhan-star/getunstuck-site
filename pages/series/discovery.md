<section class="section-wide">
  <div class="section-label">The permanent model</div>
  <h2>The Discovery Loop.</h2>
  <p class="prose" style="margin-top:.8rem;">Discovery is not a checklist. It is a loop. It ends in re-observation — not execution. Discovery hands off before the build begins, then starts again. Every episode of this series trains one node of the loop.</p>

  <div class="disc-loop">
    <div class="disc-loop-node disc-loop-node--start">Reality</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node">Observe</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node">Question</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node">Hypothesis</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node">Eliminate</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node">Constraint</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node">Frame</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node disc-loop-node--decision">Decision</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-arrow">↓</div>
    <div class="disc-loop-node disc-loop-node--loop">Observe Again ↺</div>

     THE OBJECT

<section class="section-wide">
  <div class="section-label">The permanent object</div>
  <h2>Three terms. One distinction.</h2>
  <p class="prose" style="margin-top:.8rem;">The Loop compressed into its essential form. Most organisations skip the middle term — and pay for it in execution.</p>

  <div class="disc-triad">
    <div class="disc-triad-item">
      <div class="disc-triad-term">Observation</div>
      <div class="disc-triad-def">What you can verify. What the data shows. What you saw happen.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="disc-triad-arrow">→</div>
    <div class="disc-triad-item disc-triad-item--gap">
      <div class="disc-triad-term">Interpretation</div>
      <div class="disc-triad-def">What it means. The hypothesis, the constraint, the frame. The step most organisations skip.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="disc-triad-arrow">→</div>
    <div class="disc-triad-item">
      <div class="disc-triad-term">Decision</div>
      <div class="disc-triad-def">What to do. Only as good as the interpretation that preceded it.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

     WHAT THIS SERIES COVERS
═══════════════════════ -->
<section class="section-wide">
  <div class="section-label">What the series covers</div>
  <h2>Discovery applied to four domains.</h2>
  <p class="prose" style="margin-top:.8rem;">The discipline is the same. The domain changes the texture. Thirteen episodes across the full scope of where discovery is most often skipped.</p>
  <div class="cp-vocab-grid" style="margin-top:1.5rem;">
    <div class="cp-vocab-item">
      <div class="cp-vocab-term">Organisations</div>
      <div class="cp-vocab-def">Systems, not org charts. The constraint lives in the interaction — not the function or the person.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="cp-vocab-item">
      <div class="cp-vocab-term">Customers</div>
      <div class="cp-vocab-def">Customers buy the removal of a constraint, not a product. Discovery is the only path to seeing the actual constraint.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="cp-vocab-item">
      <div class="cp-vocab-term">Strategy</div>
      <div class="cp-vocab-def">Advantage begins with seeing what others overlook. Strategy without discovery is pattern-matching against yesterday's map.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="cp-vocab-item">
      <div class="cp-vocab-term">Yourself</div>
      <div class="cp-vocab-def">The observer is part of the system. Your assumptions are an input. Discovery begins with knowing how you see.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
