# Section 1 — Governing Question
governing_question: "How does the basis of competition change over time?"

# Section 2 — The Mechanism
mechanism:
  intro: "Execution becomes abundant before executives notice. When the thing a firm was paid to do becomes easy to acquire — through technology, internalisation, or competition — the evaluation criteria shifts. The new scarce asset attracts the premium. The old asset becomes a floor, not a ceiling."
  steps:
    - "Execution becomes abundant (GenAI compresses it; GCCs internalise it)"
    - "Scarcity migrates to the next layer"
    - "Customers shift evaluation criteria before financials reflect the change"
    - "Premium migrates to the new source of scarcity"
    - "Incumbents optimise the current source of value — and fall behind"
    - "Competitive reordering completes"
  note: "The Compression Zone (execution + trust) and the Expansion Zone (influence + indispensability + ownership + growth) are not strategic choices. They are positions on a landscape that is always moving. The Crossing is the boundary between them."

# Section 3 — First Principles
first_principles:
  - "Execution becomes abundant before executives notice."
  - "Evaluation criteria change before financial results change."
  - "Trust is the highest reward the Compression Zone offers — and its ceiling."
  - "Influence begins before the buying process begins."
  - "Indispensability is not claimed. It is demonstrated through one test: would the customer create the same value without you?"
  - "Decision rights follow obligation — not contribution, not trust, not even participation."
  - "A GCC is designed to optimise one enterprise. The Expansion Zone is built on leverage across enterprises."
  - "The firms that compound over time are the ones that move before the market confirms the move."
  - "Participation earns the upside. Ownership earns the obligation."
  - "Growth earns the premium. Execution earns compensation."

# Section 6 — Open Questions
open_questions:
  - "Can value migrate backwards — from a higher layer to a lower one — and under what conditions?"
  - "How quickly does migration occur? Is it a step-change or a gradient?"
  - "Can two scarcities coexist at the same layer, or does one always displace the other?"
  - "Is The Crossing a single event or a long transition — and how does a firm know it has happened?"
  - "Does the mechanism apply in non-market systems (governments, universities, militaries)?"
  - "What determines how long an Expansion Zone advantage remains before it too becomes the new Compression Zone?"

# Evolution
evolution:
